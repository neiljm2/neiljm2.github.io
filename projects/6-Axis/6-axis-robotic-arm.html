Project Overview

This project involved building a custom 6-axis robotic arm from the ground up, starting from structural design in CAD to routing motor controller boards and writing real-time control algorithms. The primary test task was navigating the end-effector smoothly through a constrained space.

Mechanical & Electrical Build

The arm was modeled in SolidWorks, factoring in structural strength, rotational limits, and weight distribution. High-torque servo actuators drive the primary joints, powered by a customized distribution board.

Figure 2: 3D CAD schematic of structural linkages and axis configurations.

Control Architecture

The arm operates on dynamic control schemes implemented on a custom microcontroller stack:

  • Joint-Space Control: Executes rapid, collision-free moves between coordinate points.
  • Task-Space Control: Directs coordinates in Cartesian space to follow precise vector paths.
  • PID Feedback: Regulates system actuators with custom tuning parameters for optimal stabilization.
